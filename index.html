What is Financial literacy?

Financial literacy is the ability to be resourceful in budgeting, investing, credit, retirement plans, and debt management which are all essential to making important financial decisions that will change the course of an individual’s future.

Learning about money is the first step to mastering your financial future.

Investing in your knowledge today can lead to a lifetime of financial security and confidence.

Taking control of your finances isn’t just smart—it’s empowering.

Image by Finexo

Why is it important?

Very relevant in today’s globally interconnected world. Willis (2017), a Professor of Law at Loyola Law School in Los Angeles, believed that there is a growing demand and importance of financial education in the future as the complexity of taxes, investments, and mortgages continues to increase. According to Özdemir (2022), who has a Master's degree in Economics and emphasizes financial literacy education, “budget, savings, spending, investment, etc. issues are important for individuals in every period,” illustrating having a strong financial capability at an early age will always remain useful and valuable.
Willis, L. E. (2017). Finance-informed citizens, citizen-informed finance: An essay occasioned by the International Handbook of Financial Literacy. Journal of Social Science Education, 16(4), 16–30.
Özdemir, B. (2022). Financial literacy in the education process: Literature study. The Universal Academic Research Journal, 4(2), 64–70.

About Us

We are NYC public high school students, who are taking part in the Quality of Life project about Financial Literacy. We aim to create a public proposal to submit to the WISE foundation. Through this journey we hope to implement feasible actions to help the community bring more light on the lack of financial literacy knowledge among peers.

Recommendations/Proposals

  • We create this website for awareness
  • We create flyers, so people are able to see the bigger picture
  • We create petition for people to sign in order to make a curriculum change in HS around Brooklyn
  • We want to go to different classes within our school to give mini-lessons about finance, so people can apply the skill to their everyday life. This also helps fill in the gap of individual’s knowledge in financial literacy.

Challenges we foresee

  • It might be a hard time contacting people in order for our proposal to succeed.
  • It takes a lot of time to design
  • We have to be educated enough to teach
  • Have to have enough petition
  • How we may propose to fit our plan into the already existing DOE curriculum
